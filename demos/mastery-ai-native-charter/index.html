17:00 - 24:00 | SY27 Data Review

AI Native data work starts with better triangulation.

For high school, the focus is improving Math and Reading proficiency and growth. The practical move is reviewing PSSA and Keystone trends alongside MAP RIT data, Amplify units, and screening data so instructional adjustments are based on patterns, not isolated scores.

PSSA and Keystone results show the long-view trend. AI Native leadership means reading that trend beside MAP RIT growth and unit-level evidence before deciding what instruction needs to change.
SY27 Operating Rhythm | Schoolwide MTSS Tracker

The tracker turns scattered data into a schoolwide process.

Next year, every school needs a comprehensive MTSS data tracker for grades 3-12, including students with IEPs. The tracker should make Math and Reading needs visible, assign supports, and monitor whether interventions are actually moving students forward.

Required Evidence

PSSA, MAP, Firefly screening, MAP RIT scores, growth goals, Amplify unit assessments, EM2, and other interim assessment data.

Reporting Rhythm

At least four data points per reporting period, with student progress outcomes visible enough for biweekly review.

Intervention Record

Clearly named Tier 2 and Tier 3 interventions for Math and Reading, including intervention details, progress, and next actions.

Ownership

A dedicated instructional leadership team member should own the tracker, maintain quality, and prepare it for biweekly review.

Planning Link

Assistant Principals of Instruction should complete the PSSA, Keystone, MAP, and Amplify analysis soon enough to inform RP #1 QAG (Reporting Period #1 ~ Quarterly Action Goal) planning.

SLG Compliance

Student Learning Goal updates are expected at the next All-Admin meeting, with minimal changes anticipated and full team compliance still required.

AI Native does not mean more dashboards. It means a clear review cycle: identify need, assign support, monitor progress, and adjust quickly.
